Soren Physio · Visual Design Directions

Three ways the app could look

Same screens, same data, same tap-to-complete flow you're using now — just different visual personalities. Each is shown on a phone with Soren's real exercises so the comparison is honest. Pick one (or mix), and I'll build it into the live app.

Audience: 14yo, sporty Frisbee · basketball · football Gamification: later, not now Version number: subtle, bottom No build step · stays vanilla
Direction 01

Courtside

Bright, energetic, daytime

A clean light theme with a bold sports-orange header and a slim progress bar. Feels like a modern fitness app — friendly and high-energy without being childish. Easiest, most legible read in daylight.

  • Warm orange = effort / energy
  • White cards, soft shadows, big tap targets
  • Thin progress bar instead of a number
  • Safe, broadly appealing, low-risk

Direction 02

Night Training

Dark, athletic, "performance app"

The Strava / Nike Training vibe teenagers actually associate with sport. Dark UI, a single electric-volt accent, and a progress ring up top that reads as a daily target to close. Looks serious and a bit cool — less "medical homework".

  • Dark canvas, one punchy accent
  • Progress ring = a goal to close each day
  • Great battery/OLED + easy on the eyes at night
  • Naturally leads into streaks later

Direction 03

Power Up

Playful, ready to gamify

The most fun and the most future-proof. A vibrant purple gradient and an XP-style bar up top. We don't turn on streaks/points now, but the layout already has a home for them, so adding gamification later is a flip of a switch, not a redesign.

  • Bold gradient, arcade energy
  • XP bar is decorative now → functional later
  • Reserved slots for streak / level
  • Highest "want to open it" factor
